The time window for the jam was 11 days, which by the end of it I would have a game that aptly fit the wasteland theme with an optional theme of 'Radio Communications'. The base expectations of the jam were fairly low in order to allow participants freedom. The game could have been 2D or 3D, I chose to make a 3D game as I hadn't worked with it much before. As mentioned in the README, I chose Unity as my engine since I had done some work with it a few years ago, and Blender to do my modelling and texturing since I have quite a bit of experience with it. 

I spent the first night coming up with an idea that could incorporate the optional theme, and settled on something I was happy with. I decided the player would be placed in a, well — wasteland, where they would explore, activate terminals which would give them hints of story through the radio tower, and end with turning the tower on to return "home". This story is not clear in the game I have made. There are no hints of story, but the walking around and activating terminals works. The environment is not very interesting, and it's not super clear what you are supposed to do in my opinion. There are clear ways that the game can be improved, but this is what I managed to do with the time I dedicated to the jam.

Looking back, I think I would have liked to make a group for the game jam. Doing all of this alone, while needing to relearn the engine was a pain and could have been made easier by working with others. I think I also would have liked to just dedicate more time to the jam itself. I spent at least 11 hours working on it, but it's clear that more time could have done wonders. I'm glad and vaguely impressed with myself that I got a working game, but I would like to have done more. And just doing another jam would help, I spent a lot of time troubleshooting random things that I now know how to fix. I really enjoyed the jam, and will definitely be doing more in the future though!
